How to make arepas

Description

Arepas are a food found mainly in Colombia and Venezuela, as well as the diaspora around the world. Similar to pupusas, these corn patties let you make your own combination.

Ingredients

  • Masa arepa
    • Note: this is NOT the same as regular masa. It needs to be a very specific type of masa for it to work.
  • Avocado, sliced
  • Black beans, drained and rinsed
  • Queso fresco, crumbled
  • Eggs, scrambled
  • Salt
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix (by hand!) the masa arepa and a pinch of salt with water in a roughly 1:1 ratio until the texture is right.
  2. Cover the bowl with a towel and let the masa sit for 5 minutes.
  3. In a large skillet, start heating the cooking oil on high heat.
  4. When the masa is ready, start forming it into balls, then flatten them into patties about half an inch thick.
    • The dough should not be so dry it cracks but not so wet it sticks to your hands too much.
    • Add more water or more masa as needed to get the consistency right.
  5. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F
  6. Fry the patties a few minutes on each side until firm.
    • Some char marks are totally fine here!
  7. When all the arepas have been fried, put them on a baking sheet in the oven for ~15 minutes.
  8. When they are done baking, enjoy!
    1. Slice them open horizontally, creating a pocket.
    2. Fill with the avocado, eggs. black beans, and queso to your liking.
    3. Eat them and be happy :)

Feel free to experiment with other types of fillings (just never peanut butter or tomatoes)
