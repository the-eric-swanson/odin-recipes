How to make Eggplant Involtini

Description

This tasty take on the classic eggplant parmesan is a great meal any time of year, and gluten-free to boot!

Ingredients

  • 2 medium eggplants
    • Note: these should be the purple ones, not the strange little white ones for this recipe.
  • 1 large can of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 container of whole milk ricotta cheese
  • Lots of fresh basil
  • Italian seasoning
  • Hot italian peppers
  • 1 head of garlic, peeled and separated into cloves
  • Parmesan cheese to taste
  • Olive oil (lots of it!)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F.
  2. Slice the eggplant into 1/4-inch slices.
  3. Sprinkle the eggplant slices liberally with salt and allow to sit for five minutes.
    • This step gets a lot of the bitter water out of the eggplant. You will likely see them "sweating"
    • Wipe off the water at the end of the five minutes and proceed.
  4. While the eggplants are sitting, chop some basil and mix it into the ricotta, along with the Italian spices.
  5. Fry the eggplant slices in oil a few minutes per side, until they soften a bit and you can roll them. Set them aside until all are done.
  6. Lay out the eggplant slices and put a dollop of the seasoned ricotta on each one, then roll them into a medium-tight roll.
    • If you have too much ricotta, just eat whatever is left :P
    • If you don't have enough ricotta, take a little from each one and spread it around to make it work.
  7. Mix the crushed tomatoes with the hot italian peppers to taste. I like it spicy!
  8. In a large lined baking sheet, spread the tomato sauce to evenly cover the bottom.
  9. Place the eggplant rolls evenly throughout the sauce.
  10. Sprinkle the whole cloves of garlic in any open spaces left in the pan.
  11. Bake the whole thing for ~20 minutes.
  12. When they are done baking, enjoy!
    1. Grate or slice some parmesan on top for extra cheesiness.
    2. Add some more hot peppers if you need more spice.
    3. Eat them and be happy :)
